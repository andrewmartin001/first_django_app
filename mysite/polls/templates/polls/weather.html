Dunedin Weather Data

Data scraped from the Otago Uni weather station

Overview

The University of Otago physics department publishes online its weather data gathered since 2002. I have written some python to scrape this data from the web and make some interesting plots to see if the data tells us something about general trends. The code is available in Github for those interested in playing with the data themselves.

Temperatures to date

Firstly, here is the temperature in Dunedin from the start of 2017 until the end of September. The plot is interactive so you can zoom in to a paricular region of interest if you want to know what the weather was like on that day.

Yearly average temperature

Climate science tells us that the global yearly average temperature is increasing in general (over timescales of many years). From our data we can detect an increase in Dunedin temperatures between 2002 and 2016. (Although I would be careful from extrapolating too much from the small number of years from one location.) The slope of the fit has a p-value of around 0.01, meaning that if there were actually no trend one would find such a slope (or greater) by chance 1 percent of the time. This is usually deemed an acceptable risk of a false positive in a statistical test.

Total rainfall

The chart below shows the rainfall since 2003 (the rainfall records in 2002 were somehow corrupted). Each bar represents one day's worth of rain. After 2009 there seem to be occasional days with more than 50 mm rain which were not seen before. The large spike in June 2015 is on the day of Dunedin's one in 100 year flood .
